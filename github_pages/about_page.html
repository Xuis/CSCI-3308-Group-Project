Vision Statement


To provide competitive user-centered access to avian knowledge

Description


Birdbox is a digital interactive field guide meant to help in the identification of birds. Currently this database is populated with common birds from Colorado, including birds commonly found in zoos. Without Birdbox, the traditional and most realible source of avian identification is found in bulky 800+ paper field guides. This can be difficult for inexperienced users and can be damanged in the field. Birds are everywhere, but for many budding ornithologist there wasn't a clear way to translate what they saw in the field into a field guide.

Birdbox is that solution

Birdbox is a simple and user-friendly alternative to traditional methods for identification. It works by having users select a few common sources for identification. These choices were decided based on what the typical layman or even experienced users would notice when seeing a bird in the field. These are some of the most notable traits that make identification simple.
Birdbox works with a few simple steps:

Show us colors that you saw
Give us the rough size of the bird
Choose any additional notable features you think of

That's it. Start searching now!

Design Anaylsis and Breakdown


The size selection is determined by the following:


The average length of each bird in the database

The average mass of each bird in the database

The average wingspan of each bird in the databse
